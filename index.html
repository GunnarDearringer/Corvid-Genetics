Crows in Religion

Christianity +

In Christianity, crows are mentioned in the Bible as creatures God provides for, symbolizing His care for all—even those deemed “unclean.”

“Are not two sparrows sold for a penny? Yet not one of them will fall to the ground outside your Father’s care.”
— Matthew 10:29
Norse Mythology +

Odin’s ravens, Huginn (Thought) and Muninn (Memory), roam Midgard and bring back news—crows as wisdom–bearers.

“Muninn daily flies about the world. I fear for Huginn, that he come not back, yet more anxious am I for Muninn.”
— Hávamál, stanza 19
Hinduism +

In many Hindu rituals, food offerings are laid out for crows as messengers of the ancestors—symbolic “feeding” of the departed.

“May these offerings reach our forebears, carried on the wings of the humble crow.”
— traditional śrāddha mantra
Native American Traditions +

Crows are often seen as spirit-guides—symbols of transformation and insight—appearing in stories and teachings.

“When the crow calls, listen: change is coming on the wind.”
— Lakota proverb
