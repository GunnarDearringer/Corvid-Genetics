What if crows evolved intelligence like ours? Would they create gods, myths, rituals?

The Experiment

Crows are among the most intelligent species on Earth. They can recognize faces, solve puzzles, use tools, and even plan for the future. Yet despite these abilities, they’ve never developed complex social structures, religion, or mythology.

This experiment seeks to ask: What if they did? If you gave crows just a nudge—through environmental cues or engineered learning—could they develop culture? Could they pass on beliefs, invent rituals, or even worship?

Our goal isn’t to control them. It’s to observe. What emerges when you blend raw intelligence with symbolic triggers? We’re not creating gods for crows. We’re asking what gods they would create for themselves.

Crows in Religion

Christianity +

Biblical symbolism: Crows represent divine provision and humility. In scripture, ravens feed prophets and symbolize God’s care for all creatures.

Norse Mythology +

Odin’s ravens Huginn and Muninn gather information across the realms, symbolizing wisdom, memory, and prophecy.

Hinduism +

Crows are honored during Shraddha rituals as messengers of ancestors, believed to carry offerings from the living to the dead.

Native American Traditions +

Crows serve as tricksters, creators, and teachers in tribal myths—embodying transformation and cosmic insight.

Final Thoughts

If crows can create gods, myths, and society when given the spark of intelligence — what does that say about humanity's own origins?
